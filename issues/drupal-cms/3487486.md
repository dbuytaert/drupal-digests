## Summary

This issue proposed bundling an Events Calendar recipe directly into Drupal CMS, adding a FullCalendar-based interactive calendar view for Event nodes. The feature was demonstrated at the Barcelona demo using a contrib recipe but was not yet included in the distribution. After discussion, maintainers decided against shipping the recipe as part of Drupal CMS itself, opting instead to add it as a Composer `suggest` entry so site owners can discover and apply it optionally. The full bundling proposal was closed in favour of a suggests-only approach tracked in [#3487703](https://www.drupal.org/node/3487703).

## Impact

No upgrade or configuration changes required.

## Technical details

The MR introduced a new `drupal_cms_events_calendar` recipe under the Drupal CMS project. The recipe declares `drupal_cms_events` as a required upstream recipe and installs the `fullcalendar` module (`drupal/fullcalendar:^3.0`). It ships a single Views configuration (`events_calendar`) using the `fullcalendar` style plugin, exposing `title` and `field_when` (via `smartdate_default` formatter) as fields. The view is configured as a page at `events/calendar` that renders as a navigation tab alongside the existing events views. The view header includes an `add_content_by_bundle` button labelled "Add an Event". The calendar is configured with day-grid, time-grid, and list sub-views, defaulting to `dayGridMonth` as the initial display.

The existing events recipe's `composer.json` gains a `"suggest"` key pointing to `drupal/drupal_cms_events_calendar` with a short description, so Composer can surface the option to site builders without requiring it automatically.

The MR was closed without merging after `phenaproxima` raised concerns about micro-recipe proliferation ("we need to trend away from micro-recipes for distribution and maintenance reasons") and `pameeela` noted that most sites do not want a monthly calendar OOTB. The `suggest`-only resolution keeps the calendar available as an opt-in add-on without bundling it into the core distribution.

### Collaboration

The thread surfaces a clear design tension that shaped the outcome. `thejimbirch` immediately raised a maintenance concern: the contrib `events_calendar` recipe already exists, so bundling a copy would mean maintaining the same thing in two places. `phenaproxima` broadened the objection to the general principle of micro-recipes, arguing it is much easier to split a large recipe later than to merge or eliminate micro-recipes once they exist. `pameeela` added a product argument: an interactive monthly calendar is niche enough that shipping it out of the box would surprise most users. Facing that consensus, `mandclu` (the recipe's author and the issue reporter) closed the MR and redirected to the suggests-only approach in [#3487703](https://www.drupal.org/node/3487703).
