## Summary

Five core recipes that were removed in Drupal 11.4.0 as part of the Standard profile content type cleanup ([#3587118](https://www.drupal.org/node/3587118)) have been restored to the 11.x and 11.4.x codebases. The removed recipes — `article_content_type`, `page_content_type`, `article_comment`, `article_tags`, and `content_search` — were still required as dependencies by third-party recipes and site templates (such as Commerce Kickstart) that build on the standard Article and Basic page content types. Their absence caused fatal errors when those downstream recipes tried to apply. The recipes are restored but no longer applied by the Standard profile or recipe. They will not be present in Drupal 12.x, giving the ecosystem one major version to migrate away from depending on them.

## Impact

Affects module maintainers.

- **Deprecation:** Five core recipes (`article_content_type`, `page_content_type`, `article_comment`, `article_tags`, `content_search`) are restored in 11.x/11.4.x but will not be present in Drupal 12.x; recipes depending on them must migrate before upgrading.

## Technical details

The recipes were initially removed as part of [#3587118](https://www.drupal.org/node/3587118), which decoupled the Standard profile from the Article and Basic page content types. That removal broke any recipe or site template that listed one of these five recipes in its own `recipes:` dependency key, producing a fatal error at apply time because the recipe directory no longer existed in core.

The fix is a direct partial revert: all five recipe directories are re-added under `core/recipes/` on both the `11.x` and `11.4.x` branches, each containing its `recipe.yml`, the associated config files (node type definitions, field storages, field instances, entity form/view displays), and a `GenericTest.php` functional test that extends `GenericRecipeTestBase`. The `11.4.x` and `11.x` commits are identical in content. The recipes are deliberately absent from the `12.x` branch.

A comment in the thread from phenaproxima articulates the backwards-compatibility principle at play: removing a recipe from a minor release is equivalent to removing a module from core in a minor release. A follow-up issue to explore formally marking recipes as deprecated was mentioned by gábor hojtsy and catch but is out of scope here.

## Upgrade

If your custom recipe lists any of these in its `recipes:` key, it will continue to work on 11.x and 11.4.x. Plan to remove that dependency before upgrading to Drupal 12, where the recipes will not exist. For example, if your `recipe.yml` currently declares:

```yaml
recipes:
  - article_content_type
  - page_content_type
```

You will need to either inline the equivalent config actions directly into your recipe, or require and ship those content type recipes yourself, before Drupal 12.
