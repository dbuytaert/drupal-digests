## Summary

Several deprecation and exception messages in Drupal core were missing the `()` suffix on function and method names, and one message was also missing a leading space, producing malformed strings like `"__FUNCTION__is deprecated"`. These have been corrected across multiple modules so that all `@trigger_error` deprecation notices and `BadMethodCallException` messages follow the standard PHP convention of including `()` after the callable name.

## Impact

No upgrade or configuration changes required.

## Technical details

The problem was found while reviewing deprecation messages consumed by automated tooling such as Rector and the Drupal update bot. Three categories of mistakes were fixed in the same commit:

1. `__FUNCTION__ . 'is deprecated` (missing both `()` and a space before "is") in `locale.module`.
2. `__FUNCTION__ . ' is deprecated` and `__METHOD__ . ' is deprecated` (space present but no `()`) throughout the codebase.
3. `__METHOD__ . ' is not allowed'` in `BadMethodCallException` throws inside `CheckpointStorage` and `ReadOnlyStorage`.

Affected files span `core/modules/config` (`CheckpointStorage`, `ReadOnlyStorage`), `core/lib/Drupal/Core/Entity` (`EntityType`, `SqlContentEntityStorage`), `core/lib/Drupal/Core/ImageToolkit` (`ImageToolkitOperationBase`, `ImageToolkitOperationManager`), `core/lib/Drupal/Core/Recipe` (`RecipeRunner`), `core/lib/Drupal/Core/Routing` (`RouteBuilder`), `core/lib/Drupal/Core/Theme` (`ThemeAccessCheck`), `core/lib/Drupal/Core/Utility` (`Error`), and the `file`, `locale`, `taxonomy`, `user`, and `views` modules. Corresponding test assertions (`expectUserDeprecationMessage`, `assertEquals` on exception text) were updated to match the corrected strings.
