## Summary

Drupal 11 requires PHP 8.3 as its minimum supported PHP version. This was a deliberate policy decision: adoption data showed PHP version requirements are not the primary barrier for sites upgrading between major Drupal versions (contrib module readiness and budget are the main blockers), PHP 8.3 had been available for 6 to 12 months by the time Drupal 11 shipped, and the extended Drupal 10 support window gives sites time to upgrade PHP before moving to Drupal 11. Sites that cannot reach PHP 8.3 can remain on Drupal 10, which was supported until at least June 2026.

## Impact

Affects site owners.

- **Requirements:** Minimum PHP version for Drupal 11 set to 8.3 (Drupal 10 retains support for PHP 8.1+).

## Technical details

This is a plan issue that established the PHP 8.3 minimum as policy; the actual code change (setting `Drupal::MINIMUM_PHP` in `core/lib/Drupal.php`) landed in the implementation issue [#3413268](https://www.drupal.org/node/3413268), which also depended on [#3400984](https://www.drupal.org/node/3400984) switching the default GitLab CI environment to PHP 8.3.

The policy rationale was built from several data points discussed in the thread: Packagist install stats showed that roughly 60% of Drupal 9 installs were on PHP 8.1 at the time Drupal 10 required it, yet PHP versions were not widely cited as the actual upgrade blocker; a 17% share of Drupal 10 installs on PHP 8.2 just three months after both were released showed increasing adoption of newer PHP versions on new sites. The PHP release cycle also mattered: if the PHP RFC to extend the security support cycle were accepted, PHP 8.3 would remain under security support until 2027, after Drupal 10's EOL, meaning sites could stay on PHP 8.3 across both the Drupal 10 and the Drupal 11 lifetimes.

The decision crystallised around the policy xjm stated: "set the minimum PHP requirement to the highest stable PHP version available at the time beta1 of the major is released." Because Drupal 11 beta1 arrived while PHP 8.3 was the latest stable release, PHP 8.3 was the correct choice regardless of the final release window. A follow-on issue ([#3406215](https://www.drupal.org/node/3406215)) was opened to formalise this rule as standing policy for future major releases.

## Upgrade

# Upgrading to Drupal 11

Ensure your server is running **PHP 8.3 or higher** before upgrading to Drupal 11. Drupal 10 continues to support PHP 8.1 and 8.2, so you can upgrade PHP while still on Drupal 10 and then proceed to Drupal 11.

```bash
php -v   # must report 8.3.x or higher
```

Sites that cannot move to PHP 8.3 should remain on Drupal 10 (supported until at least June 2026).
