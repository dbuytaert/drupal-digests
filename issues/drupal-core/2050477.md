## Summary

This meta issue tracked the effort to ensure every Drupal core component has at least one active maintainer listed in `MAINTAINERS.txt`. Opened in July 2013, it catalogued components that were either entirely absent from the file or listed with no named maintainer, and coordinated a series of sub-issues to recruit and formally add volunteers. Over more than a decade it produced at least ten closed sub-issues covering individual modules and subsystems. The issue closed in 2025 after a formal annual check-in process was established: maintainers are now contacted each year to confirm they wish to continue, open positions are publicly advertised through a recurring child issue, and `MAINTAINERS.txt` is kept current as a result.

## Technical details

The issue operates entirely at the governance and documentation level. No source code was changed as part of this meta issue itself; all concrete file edits happened in the child issues it spawned (e.g., [#1823042](https://www.drupal.org/node/1823042), [#2048925](https://www.drupal.org/node/2048925), [#2045901](https://www.drupal.org/node/2045901), [#2054181](https://www.drupal.org/node/2054181), [#2054499](https://www.drupal.org/node/2054499), and others).

The starting inventory in the issue body divided components into three categories: "Component not listed" (originally just `datetime_range.module`), "Component listed with no maintainer" (around 32 entries at various points, including the asset library system, batch system, routing system, filter module, views_ui module, workspaces module, and several database drivers), and "Component listed with maintainer" (80 components at the time of writing).

The issue summary was revised repeatedly through comments as sub-issues were resolved, components were removed from the "needs maintainer" list, and the overall MAINTAINERS.txt structure evolved. A notable structural change referenced in the thread is [#2785891](https://www.drupal.org/node/2785891), which collapsed the distinction between modules, themes, and subsystems in the file.

The long-term resolution was anchored to governance issue [#3458223](https://www.drupal.org/node/3458223) ("Adopt renewable terms for subsystem and topic maintainers"), which introduced annual renewable terms. The check-in process, documented at the Drupal.org maintainer policy pages, involves outreach email templates, a tracking spreadsheet, and a living documentation page. After each check-in cycle, a new child issue advertises any remaining open positions to the community.
