# #3321254: [upstream] Remove Format button does not remove `style` attributes from block-level elements

## Summary

The "Remove Format" button in CKEditor 5 did not strip `style` attributes from block-level elements such as paragraphs, even when those elements carried inline styles added by pasting from Word or other sources. The button remained grayed out unless a recognized formatting element (bold, italic, etc.) was present. This was a long-standing upstream CKEditor 5 limitation that Drupal could not work around on its own. The fix shipped in two stages upstream: inline `style` removal was corrected in CKEditor 5 v38.0.0, and block-level `style` removal was corrected in v46.0.0. Drupal 10.6.x and 11.4.x, which updated to CKEditor 5 v47.0.0, include both fixes. Drupal 10.5.x and 11.2.x remain on v45.2.2 and are not affected by the block-level fix.

## Impact

No upgrade or configuration changes required.

## Technical details

The `Remove Format` button is provided by CKEditor 5's built-in `RemoveFormat` plugin. Per CKEditor 5 documentation, the plugin was originally designed to strip only inline formatting and explicitly excluded block-level formatting. As a result, `style` attributes on block elements such as `<p>` were silently ignored, and the button stayed inactive unless a recognized inline format (bold, italic, underline) was present in the selection.

The issue split into two separate upstream defects. First, inline `style` attributes were not being removed by `RemoveFormat` even from inline elements; this was tracked at https://github.com/ckeditor/ckeditor5/issues/11580 and fixed in CKEditor 5 v38.0.0 via https://github.com/ckeditor/ckeditor5/pull/13949. Second, `style` attributes on block-level elements remained unhandled; that gap was tracked at https://github.com/ckeditor/ckeditor5/issues/13983 and addressed in v46.0.0 via https://github.com/ckeditor/ckeditor5/pull/18603.

Drupal has no native workaround: the `style` attribute handling lives inside the CKEditor 5 JavaScript bundle packaged in `core/assets/vendor/ckeditor5`. Community contributors used the "CKEditor 5 Paste Filter" contributed module as a temporary mitigation for paste-from-Word scenarios. The fix became available to Drupal users when Drupal 10.6.x and 11.4.x shipped with CKEditor 5 v47.0.0. This issue was resolved as a dependency on [#3340578](https://www.drupal.org/node/3340578), the meta issue tracking prioritized CKEditor 5 upstream blockers.
