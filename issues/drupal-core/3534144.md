## Summary

Drupal 12 has finalized its platform requirements for hosting environments. The new major version will require PHP 8.5, following the policy to adopt the latest stable PHP release. Database minimums were carefully chosen to balance access to modern features with realistic hosting constraints. Most database versions remain the same or increase conservatively, recognizing that databases are harder to upgrade than PHP in many organizations. These requirements balance supporting newer platform capabilities with making Drupal 12 adoption accessible to a wide range of hosting environments.

## Impact

Affects site owners.

- **Requirements:** Drupal 12 will require PHP 8.5, MySQL 8.0, MariaDB 10.11, PostgreSQL 18, and SQLite 3.45

## Technical details

This meta issue coordinated decisions across multiple subsystems to establish Drupal 12's minimum platform requirements. The PHP requirement follows the recently adopted policy [#3406215](https://www.drupal.org/node/3406215) to require the highest stable PHP version available at beta release, which will be PHP 8.5.

For databases, the team applied different policies per platform based on their release cycles and ecosystem adoption patterns. MySQL remains at 8.0 despite that version reaching end-of-life before Drupal 12's release, prioritizing easier adoption since the security surface of database-specific features is low. MariaDB increases from 10.6 to 10.11 to follow their LTS lifecycle more closely. PostgreSQL jumps to version 18 based on the policy to require the most recent major version available six months before the Drupal major release. SQLite stays at 3.45 as there were no pressing feature needs.

These decisions considered Ubuntu LTS shipping versions, hosting provider constraints, and the balance between modern capabilities and adoption barriers. The requirements were documented and announced to give hosting providers and site owners advance notice.
