## Summary

The Drupal core community has reached a policy decision to deprecate the `stable9` base theme in Drupal 11 and eventually move it to a contributed project. Stable 9 is used on only about 2.8% of Drupal 11 sites, there will be no Stable 10 or Stable 11 (Starterkit is the recommended replacement workflow), and keeping it in core creates ongoing maintenance overhead. This issue records the policy agreement; the actual deprecation implementation is tracked in the meta issue [#3560200](https://www.drupal.org/node/3560200).

## Impact

Affects module maintainers.

- **Deprecation:** The `stable9` base theme is planned for deprecation in Drupal 11 (targeting 11.4.0) and removal in 12.0; implementation tracked in #3560200.

## Technical details

The original Stable theme was already moved to contrib per [#3309392](https://www.drupal.org/node/3309392). Stable 9 (`stable9`) was left in core at that time, but there is no Stable 10 or Stable 11 because Starterkit replaced the sub-theming workflow Stable 9 enabled. `catch` observed that `stable9` does not include any CSS or templates that core does not also ship, meaning sub-themes get a frozen snapshot that diverges from core improvements rather than an actively maintained base. Every CSS reorganization in core must be duplicated in `stable9` to keep its test suite green (e.g. [#3565297](https://www.drupal.org/node/3565297) was cited as a concrete example of this burden). Per the policy established in [#3158669](https://www.drupal.org/node/3158669) for deprecating low-usage components, usage telemetry shows `stable9` on approximately 2.8% of Drupal 11 sites, below the 5% threshold. An additional complication noted by longwave is that the Starterkit scaffold theme itself currently declares `stable9` as its base theme. The implementation of the deprecation warning and the roadmap for moving `stable9` to contrib are being carried out in the meta issue [#3560200](https://www.drupal.org/node/3560200). If the deprecation lands in 11.4.0, removal can follow in Drupal 12.0; otherwise it must wait until Drupal 13.

### Collaboration

markconroy, the `stable9` subsystem maintainer, created the issue to preserve catch's Slack discussion rather than as an endorsement, and was initially ambivalent about the proposal. Several months later, after accepting that the Starterkit workflow supersedes the minimal sub-theming pattern that Stable 9 enabled, markconroy gave explicit approval as subsystem maintainer (comment [#16252986](https://www.drupal.org/node/16252986)). catch built the case over multiple comments by citing the 2.8% usage figure and pointing to the concrete maintenance friction of mirroring CSS changes. Gábor Hojtsy signed off as Drupal core product manager, noting that all arguments pointed to deprecation being the most logical step.
